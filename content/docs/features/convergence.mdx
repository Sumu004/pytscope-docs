---
title: Convergence
description: Loss and grad-norm trend, divergence (NaN/Inf), and robust local-window spike detection.
---

The convergence analyzer watches whatever scalars you log — typically `loss`
and `grad_norm` — for the patterns that usually mean something is wrong with
*training*, not just *throughput*:

- **Trend** — is loss actually decreasing, plateauing, or diverging?
- **Divergence** — `NaN` / `Inf` values, flagged immediately with the step
  they first appeared on
- **Spikes** — a robust local-window outlier test (not a fixed threshold)
  that catches sudden jumps in loss or gradient norm without false-firing on
  normal noise

```python
prof.log(loss=loss.item(), grad_norm=grad_norm)
```

Because convergence shares the timeline with timing and memory, a spike that
*coincides* with a step-time or memory spike is promoted from "noise" to a
[cross-signal finding](/docs/features/cross-signal) — that co-occurrence is
strong evidence of a real optimization event.
