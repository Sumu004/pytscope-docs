pytscope makes quantitative claims — straggler attribution, exposed
communication, MFU. This page summarizes what's been validated on real
hardware and links to the full protocol and reports.

## Real multi-GPU NCCL run (Kaggle, 2× T4)

<Callout type="success" title="Straggler attribution — exact pass">
  `DIST.STRAGGLER` named the injected rank correctly: z = 14.1, 100%
  critical-path share vs. 50% expected by chance, 27.5% wall lost to
  imbalance.
</Callout>

<Callout type="info" title="Exposed communication — directionally confirmed">
  Plus a genuine finding about the hardware itself: on PCIe-only GPU pairs
  (no NVLink) the all-reduce is link-bandwidth-bound, so absolute exposed-comm
  time stays roughly constant across batch sizes. `DIST.EXPOSED_COMM`
  correctly fired **HIGH** for both the small- and large-batch configs (72%
  vs. 62% exposed, overlap improving in the predicted direction) — documented
  as an interconnect-topology caveat.
</Callout>

## What's exact vs. statistical

| Claim | How it's validated |
|-------|--------------------|
| Pipeline-bubble closed form | Reproduced exactly across `(p, m)` — synthetic, deterministic |
| Exposed-comm overlap math | Exact interval arithmetic, tested on synthetic traces with known answers; parser validated against a real `torch.profiler` export |
| Efficiency budget identity | Line items sum *exactly* to measured wall time — no fudge factor |
| Straggler detection | Statistical (binomial-persistence test against chance), confirmed on real hardware above |
| AutoProfiler step structure | Tested under both gradient accumulation and activation checkpointing |
